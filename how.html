How will Attender help

We believe that the features offered by Attender will battle loneliness among students, aid teachers in providing better guidance to their students and allow students to easily get in touch with with a nurse or councellor when they need help. Thus decreasing the number of students who choose to drop out of high-school.

The mentor program will facilitate a social network for new students and give students who doesn't have a lot of friends or has a hard time at home a place to go on occasional week nights. It will also create a platform where older students can keep an eye on younger students and allert the school if they notice something is wrong. It will also give students an older student to turn to when they don't want to go to a teacher or another adult, creating several paths for getting help.

The attendance data will allow students an overview of what they missed if they failed to show up to class and give them a chance to catch up on their own. This data will also help teachers to reach out to student and help them through subjects where they struggle or have fallen far behind.

The reward system will make it more compelling to go to school, and even more compelling to get back after a period of low attendance. Motivating students to come to school, even when it's not so tempting.

The chat-room where students can get in touch with either the nurse or the guidance councellor will make it easier for students to reach out when they need help. Instead of having to book an appointment and physically going to the nurse, possibly drawing attention to themselves doing so.
