Why Attender

Imagine this...

You're in your second year of high school in math class. You're sitting next to one of your classmates, Dan. Dan is a quiet guy, but he always lends you a pencil when you've forgotten your own. During the second semester, however, you notice that Dan stops showing up to math class. You ask around and it turns out Dan has stopped showing up to school all together. Dan has dropped out.

Around 30% of students in Norway drop out of high school. That's almost every fourth student. This has major consequences for their future and their ability to take an active part in society. Utdanningsforbundet states that people who have failed to complete high school generally have a lower income and are more likely to claim social benefits than people who complete high school. The reason for these drop-outs are hard to pin down, but NIFU has found that it could be connected to the requirements seeming unattainable to students with a lower grade from secondary school, lack of motivation and a difficult life outside of school. Attender aims to solve these issues.
